# Local Input Guide

Purpose: Gather quick local context before the teacher presentation.

Use this for short conversations with STEM teachers, administrators, counselors, students, or local employers. Even 5-8 conversations can make the presentation much stronger.

## Teacher Questions

1. Where are you already seeing students use AI?
2. What AI uses worry you most?
3. What AI uses seem genuinely helpful for learning?
4. Have you received any school or district guidance?
5. What assignments are hardest to protect from AI misuse?
6. What assignments could become better if AI use were structured well?
7. What would you want students to disclose when they use AI?
8. What professional development would actually help?

## Administrator Or Tech Director Questions

1. Does the district have current AI guidance or a responsible use policy?
2. Are any AI tools approved, blocked, or under review?
3. How are privacy, student data, FERPA/COPPA, and vendor terms being handled?
4. Is AI guidance being embedded in academic integrity or acceptable use policies?
5. What teacher training is planned?
6. What questions are parents asking?
7. What would you want teachers to do consistently across classrooms?

## Counselor, CTE, Or Career Pathway Questions

1. Are students asking whether AI will affect college majors or careers?
2. What local career pathways are most likely to include AI tools?
3. Are any dual credit, certification, PLTW, CTE, or apprenticeship options emerging around AI?
4. What employability skills do students still need regardless of AI?
5. What local employer partnerships could connect AI to real work?

## Student Questions

Use only if appropriate and with whatever local norms apply.

1. What AI tools have students heard of or used?
2. What do students use AI for in school?
3. What do students think is fair or unfair AI use?
4. Have teachers given clear rules?
5. What do students wish teachers understood about AI?
6. What worries students about AI and future jobs?

## Employer Questions

1. Are employees already using AI tools in your workplace?
2. What tasks are changing because of AI?
3. What skills do entry-level workers need more than before?
4. What AI mistakes have you seen or worry about?
5. How do employees verify AI-generated work?
6. What would make a high school graduate more prepared for an AI-enabled workplace?
7. Are there local internships, apprenticeships, certifications, or projects students should know about?

## Fast Poll For Teachers During Presentation

Ask by show of hands or quick form:

1. I have used AI for teaching or planning.
2. My students are using AI whether allowed or not.
3. I have clear guidance from my school or district.
4. I have changed an assignment because of AI.
5. I want more examples of responsible AI use in STEM.

## Local Examples To Listen For

- A student used AI productively.
- A student used AI in a way that bypassed learning.
- A teacher saved time using AI.
- An AI response was wrong in an interesting STEM way.
- A privacy or account-access concern came up.
- A local employer expects AI familiarity.
- A local employer worries about verification, communication, or basic skills.

## How To Use Responses In The Deck

Use anonymized comments only.

Possible slide:

"What I heard locally"

Group responses into:

- Already happening
- Teacher concerns
- Useful classroom possibilities
- Skills employers want
- Support teachers need
