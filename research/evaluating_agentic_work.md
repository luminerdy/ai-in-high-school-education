# Evaluating Expert Work Built With Agents

Purpose: Define a practical way to evaluate work produced by experts using AI agents or agentic workflows.

## Core Point

Agentic AI does not remove the need for expert judgment. It changes where expert judgment must be applied.

A better workplace framing:

> AI can increase output, but useful work still depends on requirements, domain knowledge, testing, systems thinking, and accountable quality.

The question is not:

> Did an expert use agents?

The better question is:

> Did the expert produce a result that satisfies clear requirements, passes meaningful tests, handles failure modes, and can be explained and maintained?

## What Business Actually Needs

Businesses do not need more output by itself. They need dependable outcomes:

- correct enough for the use case
- aligned to user and business requirements
- tested against realistic conditions
- secure and privacy-aware
- maintainable
- explainable
- integrated with existing systems
- monitored after release
- owned by accountable humans

Agents can help create drafts, code, plans, tests, analysis, documentation, and workflows. They do not automatically create a trustworthy system.

## Evaluation Model

Evaluate agentic work across six layers.

| Layer | Evaluation Question |
|---|---|
| Requirements | Did we define the right problem, constraints, users, and success criteria? |
| Architecture | Does the solution fit the broader system, dependencies, data flows, and operational context? |
| Verification | Did we test the result against requirements, edge cases, and realistic failure modes? |
| Evidence | Is there traceable evidence for decisions, outputs, tests, and accepted risk? |
| Human Judgment | What did the expert accept, reject, override, or redesign from the agent's work? |
| Operations | Can the result be deployed, monitored, maintained, and improved safely? |

Short version:

> Requirements, tests, evidence, ownership.

## What To Ask Experts

Use these questions when reviewing work created with agents:

1. What requirement or business outcome were you solving for?
2. What constraints mattered most?
3. What did the agent generate?
4. What did you accept, reject, or change?
5. What tests prove the result works?
6. What edge cases or failure modes did you check?
7. What assumptions remain?
8. What risk is still present?
9. What must be monitored after release?
10. Could another expert maintain this without rerunning the entire agent workflow?

## Evidence To Require

For important work, do not evaluate only the final artifact. Require a small evidence package:

- requirements or user story
- acceptance criteria
- design notes or architecture decision
- test plan and test results
- edge cases checked
- known limitations
- security/privacy review where relevant
- AI/agent use summary
- human decisions and overrides
- deployment or maintenance notes

The goal is not to create paperwork. The goal is to make expert judgment visible.

## Agentic Workflow Review Checklist

| Check | What Good Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Problem framing | The expert can explain the user, outcome, constraints, and definition of done. |
| Agent boundaries | The expert can explain what the agent was allowed to do and what required human approval. |
| Quality bar | The expert defined what good output means before accepting agent output. |
| Verification | Tests map back to requirements and include failure cases. |
| Traceability | Important decisions can be traced to evidence, tests, or expert judgment. |
| Integration | The work fits existing systems, data, processes, and teams. |
| Risk handling | Known risks are named, mitigated, or explicitly accepted. |
| Maintainability | Another person can understand and maintain the result. |
| Accountability | A human owner is responsible for the outcome. |

## How This Maps To Students

Students do not need enterprise-level process, but they can practice the same pattern:

- What problem are you solving?
- What does success look like?
- What did AI help with?
- What did you change?
- What tests did you run?
- What failed?
- What do you still not trust?
- Can you explain the final result?

This is the bridge from classroom AI use to workplace AI use.

## Useful Slide Framing

Possible title:

**AI Output Is Not The Product. Verified Results Are.**

Three bullets:

- Agents can produce drafts, code, plans, and tests.
- Experts still own requirements, architecture, verification, risk, and release.
- Evaluation should focus on evidence of quality, not evidence of effort.

Speaker line:

> In the workplace, we do not need more artifacts. We need results that meet requirements, survive testing, fit the system, and can be owned by a human expert.

## Best One-Line Summary

> Agentic work should be judged by requirements met, tests passed, risks understood, and human ownership preserved.
