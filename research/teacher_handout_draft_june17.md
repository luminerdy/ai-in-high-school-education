# Teacher Handout Draft: Practical AI Guidance For STEM Classes

## Core Question

Do not ask only:

"Did the student use AI?"

Ask:

"What role did AI play, what thinking did the student do, and what evidence shows the result is reliable?"

## AI Use Ladder

| Level | AI Role | Learning Concern |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Copy answer | Replaces thinking |
| 1 | Q&A | May help or bypass |
| 2 | Prompt/context engineering | Student directs the tool |
| 3 | Tutor | Student practices with support |
| 4 | Critic/reviewer | Student revises and evaluates |
| 5 | Workflow assistant | Student manages process |
| 6 | Agentic workflow | AI plans/tests/revises under human guardrails |

## Assignment Categories

| Category | Use When | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| AI-Prohibited | independent skill practice or assessment | no AI |
| AI-Limited | AI can support but not replace learning | disclose use |
| AI-Allowed | AI workflow literacy is part of the goal | process evidence and verification |

## Evidence Of Thinking Checklist

Use one or more:

- prompt/use log
- rough work
- assumptions list
- source check
- unit check
- lab notebook
- raw data
- test cases
- debugging log
- code walkthrough
- revision notes
- oral explanation
- rejected AI suggestions
- "what AI got wrong" reflection

## Simple Student Disclosure Starters

- I used AI to brainstorm ideas, but I selected and developed the final approach.
- I used AI to explain a concept, then verified it with class notes, data, or another source.
- I used AI to critique my draft/code/design. I accepted these suggestions and rejected these suggestions.
- I used AI to help debug. The final fix was tested by...
- I did not use AI on this task because the assignment required independent work.

## Classroom Guardrails

- Use approved tools.
- Do not enter private student data into unapproved AI tools.
- Require disclosure when AI supports schoolwork.
- Teach verification, not just prompting.
- Keep some learning moments human-only.
- Make students responsible for final work.
- Treat AI detectors as a signal for conversation, not proof.
